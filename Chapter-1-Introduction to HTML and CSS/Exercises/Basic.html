Shinwari karahi

This is a popular dish, popular among the Punjab and the Pakistanis equally, Shinwari Karahi is all about the
basic and powerful tastes. This recipe adds a very little spice to fresh meat cooked in its own juices, using only
a few ingredients. It is usually cooked in a wok like pan called karahi and eaten with hot chapati or naan.
Shinwari Karahi is beautiful for its minimal ingredients — nothing over the top masala, onion — and its pure, meat-rich essence

To make shinwari karahi you need,

  • A karahi(wok).
  • Cooking spoon
  • Cooking oil
  • chicken or mutton(the one you like)
  • Cutting board
  • Different spices

Ingredients

  1. ½ kilogram of mutton or chicken.
  2. Four medium chopped tomatos
  3. Half cup of cooking oil
  4. One tablespoon of crushed ginger
  5. One tablespoon of crushed garlic
  6. One tablespoon of crushed black pepper
  7. one tablespoon of salt
  8. one table spoon of green chilli
  9. Fresh coriander for garnish

Method

  • In a karahi (wok), heat the oil and add the ginger and smashed garlic.
  • On high heat, add the mutton and cook until it changes color.
  • Cook the tomato slices over medium heat until they become mushy.
  • Let the meat to cook in its own juices, turning at times.
  • Add the green chilies, salt, and black pepper once the oil separates.
  • Cook for a few more minutes until the meat is tender.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with naan or chapati.
